Mission

The Coalition to Organize and Mobilize Boston Against Trump (C.O.M.B.A.T.) is a grassroots, intersectional

coalition of students, artists and workers organizing to creatively resist all

forms of oppression and transform the policies, institutions and systems which give rise to social, economic and environmental injustice.

Political Principles

  • We defend the self-determination of all indigenous nations. We stand against the ongoing assault on native lives and fundamental rights.

  • We fight for Black power and Black liberation, and stand firmly against all forms of institutionalized anti-Black racism and colonization.

  • We advocate for environmental justice, clean energy and a carbon negative economy.
    We oppose our socio-economic dependence on fossil fuels and the brutal resource wars that result from that dependence.

  • We fight for the protection, dignity and respect of immigrants and refugees on a state and federal level.
    We advocate for the expansion of sanctuary campuses, cities and states to defend both
    documented and undocumented individuals and families. We oppose deportations and xenophobia – no human being is illegal.

  • We are an intersectional feminist organization that opposes all forms of patriarchal oppression. We respect the agency and bodily autonomy
    of women and gender nonconforming people who are affected by institutionalized misogyny.


  • We fight for LGBTQ+ liberation. We center the struggle of gender nonconforming people, particularly trans
    people and trans people of color. We reject all forms of institutionalized transphobia and homophobia.

  • We fight for the protection, dignity and respect of Muslim people. We stand against Islamophobia, anti-Arab racism and the “war on terror,” which
    is used as an ideological framework for the continued surveillance, repression, incarceration and torture of Muslim people.

  • We defend the sovereignty of all oppressed nations and stand opposed to the imperialism
    and neocolonialism perpetuated by global superpowers, especially the United States.

  • We are committed to the social welfare and empowerment of all working class people internationally and intersectionally. We actively encourage the
    continued growth and education of our coalition and the ongoing development of our principles.


    • Organizational Principles

  • We organize democratically among those who share our principles.

  • We are committed to maintaining political and financial independence and autonomy.

  • We create safe and accessible spaces. We actively oppose oppressive language and behavior and have zero tolerance for hate speech and behavior in violation of our principles.

  • We actively center and prioritize the voices of the marginalized people most directly affected by all forms of institutionalized oppression.

  • We discuss, debate, and criticize constructively as a community committed to a shared set of principles.
